# Comprehensive Wade-Giles to Pinyin Conversion Test Document
# This document contains examples of all Wade-Giles romanization patterns for testing.

## Part I: Dynasties and Historical Overview

The Chou dynasty established the foundations of Chinese civilization. The period saw rulers from various regions: the lords of Ch'in eventually unified China, while the earlier Ch'i and Ch'u states competed for dominance. The Sung dynasty brought cultural flourishing, followed by the Yuan conquest under Mongol rule. The Ming restored Han Chinese rule, and the Ch'ing dynasty was the last imperial house.

During the T'ang period, the capital at Ch'ang-an attracted scholars from across Asia. The Han dynasty had earlier established the civil service system. Officials like Kung-sun and K'ung-fu administered vast territories.

## Part II: Geographic Names and Postal Romanizations

The provinces of China include: Kwangsi (Kwang-hsi), Kwangtung (Kwang-tung), Fukien, Chekiang, Kiangsi, Kiangsu, Shansi, Shensi, Szechwan (also Szechuan), Hopei (Hopeh), Honan, Hupei (Hupeh), Hunan, Kansu, Kweichow, Yunnan, and Anhwei.

Major cities: Peking (Pei-ching) served as the capital, while Peiping was its earlier name. Nanking (Nan-ching) was the southern capital. Tientsin, Tsingtao, Chungking, and Sian were important centers. Canton served as the gateway for foreign trade. The western region of Sinkiang and Tsinghai bordered Central Asia. Ningsia lay along the Yellow River.

Rivers: The Yangtze (also Yangtse) and Yellow Rivers shaped Chinese geography.

## Part III: Personal Names - Aspirated Consonants (with apostrophe)

The revolutionary leader Sun Yat-sen (also known by his courtesy name Chung-shan) advocated for reform. Chiang Kai-shek led the Nationalist government. Chou En-lai served as premier under Mao Tse-tung. Teng Hsiao-p'ing implemented economic reforms.

Other notable figures: Ch'en Tu-hsiu, Ch'iu Chin, T'an Ssu-t'ung, K'ang Yu-wei, Ts'ai Yuan-p'ei, T'ao Ch'ien, P'an Ku, and Ts'ao Ts'ao.

## Part V: Diacritic Variations

The circumflex appears in forms like Ch'e (-> Che), Ch'en (-> Chen), as well as Che (-> Zhe).
The breve marks certain vowels in historical texts.
The umlaut u appears in: Chu/Chuan/Chueh/Chun and Ch'u/Ch'uan/Ch'ueh/Ch'un (-> Ju/Juan/Jue/Jun and Qu/Quan/Que/Qun).
Also: Hsu/Hsuan/Hsueh/Hsun (-> Xu/Xuan/Xue/Xun), Lu/Lueh (-> Lu/Lue), Nu/Nueh (-> Nu/Nue), and Yu/Yuan/Yueh/Yun (-> Yu/Yuan/Yue/Yun).

## Part VI: Taoism and Philosophy

The philosophy of Taoism (Daoism) emphasizes the Tao (Dao). Taoist (Daoist) practitioners follow the teachings of Lao-tzu. Taoists gather at mountain temples to meditate.
